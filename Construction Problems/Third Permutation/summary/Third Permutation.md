# Third Permutation

**CSES ID:** 3422  
**Full statement:** https://cses.fi/problemset/task/3422  
**Time limit:** 1.00s | **Memory limit:** 512MB

> Problem summary.

You are given two permutations $a$ and $b$ of $1,\dots,n$ that differ in every position. Construct a third permutation $c$ that also differs from both $a$ and $b$ at every position; any such permutation is acceptable.

## Input
First line: integer $n$. Second line: permutation $a$. Third line: permutation $b$.

## Output
Print a permutation $c$ satisfying the constraints, or `IMPOSSIBLE` if none exists.

## Constraints
- $2 \le n \le 10^5$
